About

Who

  • Participants will range from local to professional artists, at least 50% of which will be Dane County residents. Special outreach efforts will be made to include artists of color and student artists.

What

  • Justified Art! is a visual art exhibition that provides a platform for artists to contribute to the ongoing dialogue surrounding Dane County’s systemic racial inequities. Artists will be selected by a distinguished jury and will have the opportunity to showcase their work at the Overture Center for the Arts.

The curators of the exhibition, Jessica Doing and Max Puchalsky, will exercise due care in handling all work submitted.

1. The artist’s application is considered a commitment to show work submitted if selected by the jury.

2. OCA and the curators will not be held responsible for loss, damage, or theft of any work, whether such work is on display, in storage or in transit (including shipping).

3. Artist will be responsible for insuring their own work if desired. Work will otherwise not be insured while on display at the Overture Center.

4. All work must be completely dry, framed, wired, and ready for hanging, or otherwise suitably prepared for exhibition. No sawtooth hangers will be accepted.

5. Accepted works requiring special installation (orientation and/or hardware) must arrive with straight-forward instructions and hardware needed for install.

6. Any accepted work that differs from the digitally juried image may be excluded from the exhibition at the curators’ discretion.

7. The work must remain in the gallery for the entire length of the exhibition.

8. OCA and the curators assume the right to photograph any work for catalog or publicity purposes. Images of accepted work may be cropped or rotated in OCA promotional materials.

9. The date for return of work is an estimate and is subject to change based on the volume of submissions received or for other unanticipated scheduling needs.

10. A 10% commission on each work sold will be donated to [Justified Anger Coalition].

11. The artist will be responsible for all incoming and outgoing shipping costs, insurance, and related expenses, including travel/lodging costs if delivering work in person. Shipped entries must be sent in reusable containers. The curators will pack each piece in its original packing when available. Artists are responsible for arranging return shipping to the address of origin through their local UPS/FedEx/USPS or other shipping office at the close of the exhibition.





Jury




Freida W. High Tesfagiorgis

Artist, Curator, and Professor Emeritus of Art and African-American Studies

University of Wisconsin-Madison

High is a painter, art historian, and visual culturalist. Her expertise is modern/contemporary art, visual culture, and art theory, with emphasis on contemporary Africa and the African Diaspora (Northern Hemisphere), and modern European Art and Primitivism. Feminism and critical race theory permeate her work. She has exhibited, curated exhibitions, and published, spanning the boundaries of artist and scholar.

Linda Mathis-Rose

Artist and Retired Arts Educator
Linda MR LLC / Madison Metropolitan School District

Linda Mathis-Rose began her teaching career in 1984 and recently retired as an art teacher from the Madison school district in June of 2013. She was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania in 1958.

Starting with my father's tutelage and later the tutelage of other great folks, I fell in love with art. In 1966 my mother moved the family to the housing projects in Chicago, Illinois - where I began my journey as a freelance artist. I began working on portraits. My first commissioned oil portrait was for my great grandfather in 1968. My grandfather surprised me when he said that my work was “Good enough to start freelancing.” I felt so confident; I continued to paint portraits for many individuals.

In 1978, I received an associate’s degree in commercial art and illustration from The American Academy of Art in Chicago. I also moved to Madison, Wisconsin to further my education and life as an artist. I went on to attend the fine arts and education programs at University of Wisconsin-Madison. I received a B.S. in Art in 1982, a B. Education in 1984, and finally a Masters of Art Education in 1998.

Leslie Smith III

Artist & Assistant Professor of Painting and Drawing
University of Wisconsin-Madison

A Native of the Washington DC metropolitan area Leslie Smith III is an oil-based abstract painter. He received his Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from Maryland Institute College of Art and His Masters of Fine Arts degree in Painting and Printmaking from Yale University School of Art. Smith’s studio practice is concentrated around employing abstraction to communicate the nuance and poetics of human experiences. He is currently an Assistant Professor of painting and drawing at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. His works have exhibited in New York City, Philadelphia, Minneapolis St. Paul, and San Francisco. Most recently at Beta Pictoris: Maus Contemporary gallery, Contemporary Art Museum Houston, Milwaukee Art Museum and Madison Museum of Contemporary Art. Currently in his fifth year at UW Madison he continues to paint while continually challenges himself to make paintings that engage audiences in meaningful ways.
