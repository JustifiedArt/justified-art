About


Justified Art! is a platform for visual artists to participate in the conversation inspired by "Justified Anger", a Cap Times essay by Rev. Alex Gee detailing his personal experience with racial profiling and discrimination as an African American living in the Madison area.

You are invited to further the discussion Rev. Gee initiated through original, creative expression. Share your personal experiences with discrimination, examine your own role in perpetuating oppressive practices, or even imagine Dane County's possible future - will we ever become the flourishing, multiracial community we aspire to be? At the very least, we seek works of art that intervene and raise awareness about the racial disparities that exist in our community. More ambitiously, we seek art that intends to change the very way we see, enact, and make sense of social justice, including what we understand to be a just society itself. Insights and relationships emerging from this visual dialogue affirm both the role of art, in imagining a more equitable society, and the gallery, as a space to embody this inclusive ideal.

If selected by the jury, you will exhibit your work at the Overture Center for the Arts, Gallery I from March 10 - May 31, 2015, with a public reception on Friday, May 1 from 6-8pm during Gallery Night.

Deadline
The deadline for submission is 11:59 pm CST on Thursday, Nov 20 2014.

Eligibilty
There is no entry fee, and all artists are encouraged to apply. In order to promote a dialogue inclusive of historically marginalized perspectives as well as local voices, special consideration will be given to African American artists, other artists of color, young artists, and current residents of Dane County (and/or those with ties to the community).

To learn more about the issues, follow the resource links at the bottom of the page.

Justified Art! is co-curated by Simone Doing and Max Puchalsky. Additional help provided by Digital Handyperson, Nick Lyell.

Jury



Freida W. High Tesfagiorgis

Artist, Curator, and Professor Emeritus of Art and African-American Studies

University of Wisconsin-Madison

High is a painter, art historian, and visual culturalist. Her expertise is modern/contemporary art, visual culture, and art theory, with emphasis on contemporary Africa and the African Diaspora (Northern Hemisphere), and modern European Art and Primitivism. Feminism and critical race theory permeate her work. She has exhibited, curated exhibitions, and published, spanning the boundaries of artist and scholar.

Linda Mathis-Rose

Artist and Retired Arts Educator

Linda MR LLC / Madison Metropolitan School District

Linda Mathis-Rose began her teaching career in 1984 and recently retired as an art teacher from the Madison school district in June of 2013. She was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania in 1958.

Starting with my father's tutelage and later the tutelage of other great folks, I fell in love with art. In 1966 my mother moved the family to the housing projects in Chicago, Illinois - where I began my journey as a freelance artist. I began working on portraits. My first commissioned oil portrait was for my great grandfather in 1968. My grandfather surprised me when he said that my work was “good enough to start freelancing.” I felt so confident; I continued to paint portraits for many individuals.

In 1978, I received an associate’s degree in commercial art and illustration from The American Academy of Art in Chicago. I also moved to Madison, Wisconsin to further my education and life as an artist. I went on to attend the fine arts and education programs at University of Wisconsin-Madison. I received a B.S. in Art in 1982, a B. Education in 1984, and finally a Masters of Art Education in 1998.

Leslie Smith III

Artist & Assistant Professor of Painting and Drawing

University of Wisconsin-Madison

A Native of the Washington DC metropolitan area Leslie Smith III is an oil-based abstract painter. He received his Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from Maryland Institute College of Art and His Masters of Fine Arts degree in Painting and Printmaking from Yale University School of Art. Smith’s studio practice is concentrated around employing abstraction to communicate the nuance and poetics of human experiences. He is currently an Assistant Professor of painting and drawing at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. His works have exhibited in New York City, Philadelphia, Minneapolis St. Paul, and San Francisco. Most recently at Beta Pictoris: Maus Contemporary gallery, Contemporary Art Museum Houston, Milwaukee Art Museum and Madison Museum of Contemporary Art. Currently in his fifth year at UW Madison he continues to paint and continually challenges himself to make paintings that engage audiences in meaningful ways.
